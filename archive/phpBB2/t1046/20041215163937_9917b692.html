Yes, we are seriously desperate to hear some new Avalanches material. A way to ease your abstinence is to listen to the Japanese duo called Handsomeboy Technique. They have an EP out on the Japanese label Second Royal (www.secondroyal.com) which is called "Season of Young Mouss EP". It's fantastic.

3 tracks:

1. "Season of Young Mouss"
House beat. A loop of The Fun & Games' "Close to Carmel". In flies old school 80s rap (sounds like Grandmaster Flash or something like that). A female vocal line which almost comes straight out of the best j-pop from downtown Shibuya makes out the chorus.

2. "Our Blessings"
Phil Spector goes breakbeat. In flies Jackie DeShannons "Everytime That You Walk in the Room". Out of nothing comes strings from Stu Philips soundtrack to "Follow Me". Crescendo. Coda.

3. "Affections"
Soft beat. Soft rock background (Lettermen's "Mary's Rainbow") with a nice strings attached.

Easily the 12" of the year!

More in the same vein can be found on Losfeld's album "Gospel!" (out on the Japanese label Escalator).

And Brits go bonkers to Lemon Jelly. And Mylo's "The Valley of the Dolls".

And the US may be aware of The Busy Signals 1st album released a couple of years ago? Very uneven, but it has some golden moments very hiphop and breakbeats meet Free Design, Chris Montez etc.
