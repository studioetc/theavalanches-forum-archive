Saw Dexter play last night, so i thought i'd post a review (well, needless to say it was incredible). Jimmy played about an hour by himself of mainly housey stuff with some dnb. Then he handed over to Dexter, who strated with something i recognised but don't know the name of (lyrics: "I hope i hope i hope it's me..."). But it got off to a bad start when the turntable jammed and the record started skipping. I thought it was part of the set at first until he stopped the music and shouted "IT'S FUCKED!" at the top of his voice, to the amusement of a few people. Luckily it got going again.

He played his Hendrix skit, at least five Outkast tracks during the set (including a mix of BOB and Andy C BodyRock), some Snoop Dogg and Dre (plus a bootleg of Forgot about Dre), a great mash-up of Gravel Pit with Around the World over the top and then mixing in Rose Rouge by St Germain (this was one of the highlights). There was quite a bit of reggae, funky kingston, NERD, I Want you Back (got a great reaction), Could You be Loved, Windowlicker, Squarepusher and loads of other hip hop, electro and samba records that i can't remember. He played a few bits off gimix (odb, roy ayers, kid creole, close to you), a few Missy Elliot tracks (a cool mix of Gossip Folks with Fisherspooner and a dnb Get ur Freak On), but best of all was a mash-up of The Police' Roxanne, Doom's Night, Cyndi Lauper's Time after Time and Rick James' Superfreak. The crowd went ape when Doom's Night kicked in over Cyndi Lauper. Jimmy was dancing behind Dexter for most of the set too, getting the crowd going. He reminded me of Dancin' Homer from that Simpsons episode... He finished his set with Curtis Mayfield Move on Up, but didn't play an encore (the fault of the club more than anything).

I got quite near the DJ booth so i got to see his fingers at work. It amazed me that he was only using 2 turntables (and occasionally a cd player), i thought he used at least 3. The only downside was he didn't play his Swingset/Louis Armstrong piece, i NEED to SEE him do that, but alas. And there was some arse in a white vest (looked like someone from Home and Away) who insisted on jumping around on top of everyone, crushing people forward, elbowing them in the head, knocking drinks out of peoples hands and at one point crashing into the dj booth, which pissed Dexter off considerably. He was asking for a kick in the bollocks.

All in all it was a great night. The drinks were overpriced and i've still got tinitus from having my head about an inch away from the speakers, but sometimes these things just don't matter.
