so you might wonder how was the Fuck Clear Channel Tour show. but i wont even give you time to ask, i am going to tell you right away....
the show starts at around 9:30 at the ottobar in baltimore. first on the mic was MacLethal from Kansas city misouri. that was M.F. great opener. he rocked the house like i have never seen an opening act do. he is really nice too and promised to deliver the goods to my radio station as soon as he gets back home from the tour. really funny and witty guy. next were Grand Buffet. what can anyone say about these two? you really need to see them live cause they just killed us with laughter. don't miss them if they come around your neck o' the woods. after that was the main man himself, Sage Francis with his newly formed band, the GimmeFund (not just cancer kids need money). they played a lot of the Hope album and PJ with the two guitars, Joey Beats on a drum machine and one of the guys also had a harmonica. Sage was wearing a suit and the other guys too, joe was wearing a 70's pimpish outfit. good looking blokes really. then they exited and sage and joe got back on stage with some bling bling and a cd player. they fucking killed the rest of PJ and Hope and some unreleased tracks. it was umm umm good! some fuckheads were being asses and were requesting songs while sage was trying to lay some spoken words down so he never did. that was the part that sucked. plus i wanted xaul zan to have a little thing too but he never came out of the closet. oh well. but in over all it was an excelent show. i was talking to sage before the show and his voice really did suck and he was hoping it wloudl be cool while on stage and it was. so we were all happy. if the tour comes around your town, go see it!

ok i've been to two great gigs in the last week so i reckon i'll add my bit to this thread:

last thursday was a mini festival at electrowerks in london called 'hot trees'. The main attraction for me was Ulrich Schnauss (who was excellent) but i saw a bunch of other bands that i think other people on this board might like.

aidan smith played a short set with just him on piano and a guy alternating between trumpet and guitar. he's on twisted nerve and has the wit and tunes to follow badly drawn boy onto bigger things.

mountaineers are all over the shop (nearest comparison i can think of is the bees) but definitely worth checking out. edan is one of the members of fridge (alongside fourtet) and, whilst i can't remeber what he sounded like, i remember it being good

the other gig i saw was big boi of outkast at electric ballroom on monday night. now that was superb. killa kela supported and, having not seen his one man human beatbox set before, i was damn impressed.

he put on a proper show - cutmaster swift on the decks, two guitarists, backing singers and, best of all, four massive guys in camoflage jump suits providing coreographed dance moves! bubba sparxx was the guest and did deliverance & ugly (during which he stripped off to reveal belly and arse crack....nice!)

big goi played an hour set of old and new - so fresh, miss jackson, ghetto muzik, unhappy and a storming encore of BOB. It really didn't matter that Andre wasn't there.

i went to see the strokes last night at a small club here in columbus. the show sold out weeks ago and the crowd was abuzz. i wasn't overly excited about the show going in because their second record hadn't really clicked for me and i wondered if they'd be good, but i'm sure glad i went.

singer julian casablancas was very chatty and seemed to be in good spirits. after about four songs he said "well, that's it for our setlist. we didn't get a setlist together in time and decided to just come out here and play anyway."

so they played for another hour, kinda making it up as they went along, and it was a fun ride. they played plenty of songs off their records, but also worked in some interesting covers. first they did a half a pearl jam cover, a bit of a piss take, then casablancas said, "we're in ohio so fuck it, let's do a guided by voices cover." and they did. (don't ask what song -- i'm not much of a gbv fan.)

then a few songs later casablancas asked if the crowd would like to hear a clash cover. in the back of my mind i hoped for "clampdown," since it's an old fave (and the one i was lucky enough to get on the london booted comp). sure enough, the drummer started the beat and i recognized it at once. they fucking played clampdown. and it was pretty damn cool.

i'd forgotten how much they remind me of the velvet underground live. they slow down some of their songs, and while that often leads to a laggy-draggy effect, the strokes manage to make it work. comes off more of a simmering kinda thing. very cool.

saw automato, diverse and RJD2 last week here in columbus (which is where RJ's from, though he now lives in philadelphia).

it was held at a venue owned by the university (ohio state) and there was no alcohol sold. so it made for a decidely un-festive atmosphere. no one danced or anything. almost felt more like a music seminar than a show.

RJ was good but not great. had a sampler and four turntables, though he rarely if ever had more than two turntables going at once. he played remixed and scratched-up versions of lots of stuff from deadringer and the new record, plus a handful of other tunes, including an eric b and rakim remix that was only so-so and "let the good times roll" by the cars (but didn't segue it into his own "let the good times roll" tunes like i thought he might.) he had some of the vocals that he used on his records pressed to vinyl so he could scratch with them, and that was nice. he also pecked out stuff on the pads of his sampler, which was kinda fun. a couple times he seemed to have whole tracks programmed on the sampler, which seemed a lil like cheating. he did a lot of scratching throughout, and did a lot of scurrying between the four turntables and his record crate.

as for the opening acts ... Diverse was two MCs and a DJ named nick nack (or knick knack, or whatever). was very mediocre overall. the MCs were ok but nothing great, and the dj was average at best. he did fine playing records for the mcs to rap atop, but the "solo" he did featured the most unimpressive scratching i've seen since i was in junior high. it almost was a parody of bad scratching, really.

i only caught three automato songs but i really liked them. a four piece band plus two singers and they had a very cool groove going. i hope i get a chance to see them again.
