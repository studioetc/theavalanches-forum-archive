as far as other groups that sound like the avalanches, that's tough to say....I think they have a very unique sound (which is rare these days)...ironically, that sound is compiled almost entirely from other musics and sounds...but anyways...

as a devotee of sampled beats for about eight years now, i came across quite a few avalanche influences prior to gettin turned onto them

I would encourage you to check out DJ Shadow and Cut Chemist. Shadow is also a sample-based artist, though his arrangements are usually a bit more dark and cerebral...although on UNKLE he did do some original scoring with James Lavelle.

I specifically recommend Brainfreeze and Product Placement if you want some dope rare groove sample shit...
Shadow and Chemist arranged these albums by mixing old soul 45's continuously for over an hour....however these guys tend to replace the disco-whimsical and humorous nature of the avalanches with more grounded foundations in funk and soul conciousness and appreciation. theres also not as much popular/recognizable samples like the avos use in their live radio sets...but thats part of their party-appeal.

But with those albums, as with the avos, you get the same "where the fuck did they get that?" appeal...with some very impressive deep-crate diggin'...

check 'em out.

cheers,
the audiophile
