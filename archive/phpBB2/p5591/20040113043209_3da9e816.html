Hi Whitt (Good to touch base)

The Jean Jacques Perrey album "Mood Indigo" came out in 1970 (Vanguard LP VSD 6549) and, and in a round-a-bout way, is one of the nerdiest Moog synth albums from that period (and there were many).

Anyway, a lot of the record has some crazy sci-fi space sounds all played from those early synthesisers, but one track on this album has been pivotal in forming the shape of many hip hop and big beat tracks of the past. JJP was an early pioneer of space funk.

The track "E.V.A." is a undisputed killer! Gangstarr first sampled it on "Just To Get A Rep" and since then it has been sampled by many hip hop/electronic artists/pop over the years (Dr.Octagon, A Tribe Called Quest, House Of Pain, it has even been remixed by Fatboy Slim 5 years ago, cashing in the big beat craze, as many were pilfering it's cosmic bassline, intro-ed drum break and spacey synth trills). It is proto-trip hop in every way!

The track can be found on many compilations ("Super Breaks" on BGP records from UK being one) but the whole album is on remastered CD now, though I wouldn't recommend it, as the rest is cheese city!

Alongside all the library music that has been sampled over the years, "EVA" remains an awesome track on its own. Hence, Fatboy, The Chemical Brothers, DJ Premier, etc al, include it as a staple part of their DJ sets.
