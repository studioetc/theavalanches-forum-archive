Yeah I went, I'd got four tickets but two mates pulled out and the tickets couldn't be foisted on anyone.

It was alright, but in comparison to Platsic People, The Social was full of people not interested in the music, and there really wasn't much of an atmosphere. Jollymaking wasn't helped by the fact that I was there with only 1 mate, but we ended up having a bit of fun. James' set was pretty much the same as what we'd heard at Plastic People, but not many people were dancing except at the end. Prior to that, there was once guy dancing who just looked like he would've edanced to anything they played, and one fat fucking sweaty drunk-as-fuck pig of a guy who confounded my expectations by failing to get kicked out by the suprisingly cool bouncer (although he was very strict with 'last dance' time. There certainly weren't any special guests like the Chems or Norman, but one of the other DJs was a guy who also played at Plastic People who I had thought was pretty cool, though he failed to play Super Furry Animals' 'Slow Life' this time despite my begging.

All in all, worth going to (oh yeah, got a free beer with entry!) but frustrating and dissapointing because of lack of mates and the crowd not listening to the music.
