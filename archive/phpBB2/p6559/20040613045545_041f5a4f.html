Heyya..!

For anyone else who went to Coachella, what did you think?

I only made it to day 2, but some quick thoughts:


    prefuse 73 - I had never seen Scott play live and I have to say that this was my biggest disappointment. I'm a drummer, and while I have nothing but respect for the musicians that got up there to play, the live drummer drove me completely NUTS. The first few tracks were from O.W.E. and it seemed like, in addition to the kit drummer, there were some snare hits at all the same places from the synths and they were RARELY matched. In addition to this, it just felt like someone was playing along with the album to a standard 4/4 beat -- like you might do listening to your favorite band on your headphones. I felt like this took all the cutting syncopation and beat slices that are a prefuse trademark and doused them with heavy 4/4 unexcitement, in addition to be outta time. Whew.

    crystal method - I actually left prefuse after 3 songs, so maybe they improved... but was plesantly surpised to see crystal method playing a live set. it was worth it, even considering the incredible man-sweat humidity of that tent! what can i say.. the variances from the studio recordings, if they were there, were not very sophisticated. no big changes. but i'm a sucker for those crystal method fat beats and having never seen them play, this was a nice (guilty?) pleasure.

    2manydjs - excellence as expected, and i didn't realize how tight they would be live. (my first time seeing them live as well.) the 2-man mixing and knob twisting was quite a sight. compare this set, with zero fuckups, to donald glaude's 4-on-the-floor set before. didnt catch much of that, but the mistakes were noticable. props.

    basement jaxx - didnt realize how many singers these guys bring around, but i guess when you have a hit on the order of where's your head at, you get to tour with more people. pretty nice set.

    !!! - fun set, what can i say, will have to check these guys out more..



thats all! just off the top of my head, hope my prefuse rant wasnt too bitchy, but i'm really curious if anyone else noticed this. cmon, had to have.

cheers..

Basement Jaxx  Was the highlight of the weekend for me!! Much tighter than other live sets Ive downloaded from them. Had a mash-up about ½ way thru the set highlighted by Seven Nation Army/In Da Club. Great visuals too on a neon board behind them. Dizzee Rascal was a highlight during their set. Much better served in a guest spot than watching his full set which got a little tired

Broken Social Scene was GREAT. Played a very loose set bringing members of Metric up to play certain songs.

2 Many DJs tore up the Sahara tent. Took about 10 minutes to set up so the Glaude audience had thinned the crowd. But once 2 Many DJs started up, the tent was full in no time. Interesting to note many DJs from backstage moved to the side of the stage to watch them in action
Seb Fontaine played a good set too.

4th time seeing Radiohead & despite Thoms fragile voice, it was their best show Ive seen. Dedicated Creep to the Pixies. Thom said In college it was the Pixies & REM that changed my life.

Beck was a last minute addition so they jacked him in this tiny tent in the blistering heat. Not worth it

Pixies performance was historic. Sounded like they had never stopped touring

Atmosphere was fierce despite his DJs records warping from the sun. Everytime the record would skip, the DJ would tear it off & Frisbee it into the crowd

& props to DJ Riko for working his "California" magic out there. Had a great time man!!

ok, here's my long-winded recap. 'twas great to see crip again, and i'm glad the "california" magic came together

overall i had a blast this year. it was my fourth coachella, and definitely one of the best. it was sold out for the first time ever, with 50,000 people in attendance both days, but it almost never seemed all that crowded. the traffic in and out was bad (but we dodged most of it thanks to a super-close hotel room) and the food lines got long around 6 p.m., but otherwise the crowd was manageable.

the heat was an issue this year, with temps topping 100 both days. we stayed in the shade as much as we could, but it was hot even in the shade. we went later on sunday to avoid some of the heat. that meant i missed !!!, who i really wanted to see, but i wasn't up for standing in the hot sun to watch them, especially with so many other great acts on that day's bill.

i've got pictures and some video clips (very poor sound, but interesting viewing) in my slsk shares. anyway,

here are some highlights...

Saturday:

Section Quartet -- a string quartet that played rock tunes like led zep and the darkness. was a nice opener, but i don't think they were as good as kronos quartet, who does a similar act.

Junior Senior -- one of the highlights. they complained about the heat, saying it never gets that hot in scandinavia, but they had loads of energy and were high on the fun quotient. they played most of their album, and did a cover of Twist & Shout (with a verse of Push It thrown in) for junior's birthday. har mar superstar joined them for that tune, but didn't sing or anything, just danced around on stage. fred schneider also was on hand.

Beck -- they had him in the small Gobi tent and it was overflowing with people. it was just him with a guitar and a drum machine, and the sound was too thin to carry to the back of the overflow crowd where we were. watched a half a song and headed back to junior senior.

LCD Soundsystem -- man these guys rocked. the rhythm section was very tight and the singer was a decent front man. i hadn't heard most of the songs they played, but really liked them. we had to leave during there set cuz mrs riko wanted to see the pixies. i wish i could've seen it all.

Pixies -- i was never much of a fan and their show did little to win me over. they sounded fine, but weren't anything special for me. after about 15 minutes we went over to see ...

Stereolab -- it had just gotten fully dark when they played, and they were in one of the tents. the lights were very low, and it brought an intimate feel to things. had a cool jazzy vibe going, with a trombone and french horn, that i enjoyed. was good to finally see them in person.

The Rapture -- rocked it. worked the crowd up well and people went nuts for "house of jealous lovers," which never sounded better.

Radiohead -- i think radiohead is a great band and they do a great live show, but i'd seen them about nine months ago and had seats in the 15th row for that. this time we were way back in the crowd and thom yorke's voice sounded croaky. they had the same stage setup as they did last year. all this added up to a feeling that i was seeing something i'd already seen, but this time not as good. after a few songs, i started to hear sound coming from the other outdoor stage, some funky-ass basslines and beats, with nice "wicky wicky" scratching and some good rapping. it was Eyedea & Abilities, and we left Radiohead to check them out.

Eyedea & Abilities -- i'd never heard these guys before but they were quite entertaining. just a dj and mc, but a good show. the dj, abilities, did some cool scratching and stuff, and the mc had excellent flow. it was funky-fresh for a good 25-30 minutes, then it started to get boring. the dj did a long scratching session, and it degenerated into scratch wank, with him just scratching super fast but not generating any real rhythm or grooving. still, i was glad we left radiohead to check it out.

DJ Icon, Peretz, Seb Fontaine, The Evens, ... Trail of Dead, Danny Howells -- all were fine, but nothing to get overly excited about.

Sunday:

Donald Glaude -- good dj set, very energetic and really was into the music.

2ManyDJs -- this is what i'd been waiting for, having never seen them, and they didn't disappoint. the brothers brought out a cd player and an armload of records (no crate or anything), had some technical difficulties getting things rolling, but then the show began. they opened with green velvet "la la land" acapella atop led zep "immigrant song," and it was smokin. other tracks included the cult - she sells sanctuary, that chemical bros song with the really deep bass dive, felix - shower scene, milkshake, rapture - house of jealous lovers, crazy in love vs lumidee's "uh oh" song, a hot nirvana boot, scissor sisters - comfy numb, max romeo - chase the devil, lots more. it was fun to watch them work, usually with one brother digging through the stack of records (such a smallish stack, it was surprising how much time they spent rifling through it) while the other tweaked the eq, etc., then switching positions. didn't seem to be a whole lot of communication between them, like they know what to do without talking about it. it was less a tag-team kinda thing and more a two-headed, four-armed dj, with many times where both of them were tweaking the mixer knobs at the same time. they seemed to be having a great time, often smiling at each other's selections from the record pile, and the crowd was super pumped. it was in the big dance tent, the Sahara Tent, and crowds were generally enthusiastic there, but there seemed to be extra buzz for these guys.

Freeland -- poor guy came on after 2manydjs and while his set was nice, the crowd was as thin as i saw it in that tent all weekend.

Dizzee Rascal -- i know live rap usually is disappointing, but thought dizzee might rise above. he didn't. it was cool to see him and all, but it got old fast.

Air -- i was happy they played a lot of older tunes dating back to Moon Safari. the last time i saw them was on the 10,000 khz legend tour, and they played only one song that wasn't on that album. this time they played a friendlier set, and it was good.

BRMC -- the singer had problems with his guitars, with two of them breaking strings, so he grabbed an acoustic and started singing unplugged versions. sometimes this kind of seat-of-pants thing can be very cool, but this time it didn't quite click. they eventually got the guitars fixed and the group sounded much better electric.

Flaming Lips -- i've seen these guys six or seven times dating back 10-plus years, and they always have fun surprises. this time it was singer wayne coyne getting into a giant clear ball and walking out atop the crowd. it was a fun gimmick.

Basement Jaxx -- prolly my favorite of the live acts. they had a string of guest singers come out for different tunes, and dizzee rascal joined them for lucky star. i'd hoped he'd stick around and sing jump n shout, but it wasn't to be. they played good luck, cish cash, red alert. they did romeo but played the clash's magnificent seven music to it, just like in the soulwax bootleg. very cool. they did a brief dj set in the middle, playing a white stripes/50 cent bootleg and their pointer sisters "all systems underground" remix. ended with a rousing Where's Your Head At, which included at least five people in gorilla costumes bouncing around the stage. i have a good video clip of that in my slsk shares.

The Cure -- still sounded good after all these years.

urp, that's it. can't wait til next year.
