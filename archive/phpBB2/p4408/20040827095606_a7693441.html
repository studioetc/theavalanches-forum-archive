I am feeling bored so here goes....

"Since I Left You" contains-
============================

Elements from "Everyday" by the Main Attractions.
Samples from "Let's Do the Latin Hustle" by Klaus Wunderlich
Embodies Portions of "By the Time I Get to Phoenix"

"Stay Another Season" contains-
===============================

Elements from "Cinnamon and Clove" by Sergio Mendez
Samples from "Holiday" by Madonna

"Radio" contains-
=================

Elements from "Fat City Strut" by Mandrill

"Two Hearts in 3/4 Time" contains:-
===================================

Sample from "Yu-Ma" by Marlena Shaw

"Flight Tonight" contains-
==========================

Sample from "Wicked she Wicked" by Billy Boyo
Emodies Portions of "More Than U Know" by Prince Paul feat.
De La Soul

"Close To You" contains-
========================

Embodies Portions of "Tell Me When You Need it Again" by The
Isley Brothers
Elements from "Stool Pigeon" by Kid Creole and the Coconuts

"Diner's Only" contains-
========================

Sample from "Sparkle" by Camplo

"A Different Feeling" contains-
===============================

Sample from "Tammy" by Debbie Reynolds

"Electricity" contains-
=======================

Sample from "Rapp Dirty" by Blowfly

"Tonight" contains-
===================

Elements from "Tonight May Have to Last Me All My Life" (Which is a beautiful sentiment) by Nancy Wilson

"Frontier Psychiatrist" contains-
=================================

Elements from "Frontier Psychiatrist" by John Robert Dobson
Embodies a Portion of "My Way of Life" by Bert Kaempfert,
Carl Sigman and Hernert Rehbein

"Little Journey" contains-
==========================

Elements from "Midnight Voyage" by Mama Cass with The Mamas
& the Papas
Sample from "Holiday" by Madonna

"Live at Dominoes" contains-
============================

Sample from "Ma Baker" by Boney M
Sample from "Division 5" by 5th Dimension

"Extra Kings" contains-
=======================

Elements from "Let Me In" by The Osmonds



Phew, well there you go.

I have been digging for Avalanches Samples for 2+ years, been through a lot of obscure records. Here is some info I think might be helpful to fellow samplespotters:

Hopefully this isn't old information, but I've found:

Since I Left You samples the very quiet drum/guitar intro of :
Sly and the Family Stone's "Family Affair"

Two Hearts in 3/4 Time : samples Marlena Shaw's voice "Yu-Ma" from the "Sweet Beginnings" album. Yes people do know this one, but the Album name is not spoken of too often...

Pablo's Cruise samples a Fog Horn and Sea Gulls both of which come from a Weird record Entitled "Voice of the Sea" (5011 A) on a Cook label, made between 1952-1960. If someone wants more information on this record let me know.. it's pretty weird... the sound effect also lead into Frontier Psychiatrist


And here are some things to help people searching for samples like I am

According to an old review online Frontier Psychiatrist may sample bits from soundtracks to : "Laurence of Arabia" sp? and John Waters' "Polyester"

At the beginning of Two Hearts in 3/4 Time there are two voices saying "money" and the french word "magnet" (magnet is pronounced mah- nyay). There are numerous instructional french language albums, so if you see one, and really want the sample... good luck.. I have about 5 different ones, but never bothered to listen to them.

Also the female voice on Extra Kings is saying "j'ai soif" and "j'ai bois" both short french phrases meaning "I'm Thirsty" and "I Drink"
so if people do boolean searches, or find titles with those words in them it might be a hint. To me it sounds like a record of a Commercial for a French Soft Drink or something.

Anyway, I've got other ones i'm researching because i too am a geek

Hi people, This has to be the oldest thread in the history of forums! Anyway, here's a couple of samples that i've noticed along the way:-

Frontier Psychiatrist:- The part at the end when the woman says "yes, some birds are funny when they talk, can you think of anything else" and the girl replies "a record". The music then goes into a silly guitar break just before the next track. That sample is taken from an album called "Stereo Musical Showcase" with the track being "the Blue Danube" on Polydor from 1966.

Also, (this ones a bit of a film-spotter sample), and you have to listen very carefully to the GIMIX mix album, but they sample Robert DeNiro from the film "Midnight Run" saying something along the lines of "I'll have a glass of your finest Champagne". Midnight Run is a cracker of a film - and probably the best comedy DeNiro has done.
