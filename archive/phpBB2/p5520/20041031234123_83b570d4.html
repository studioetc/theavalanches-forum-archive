OK, here's my attempt at starting a thread where people give reports on live shows they attend. i always like hearing about current tours/shows/events, so i hope others will play along.

i'll start with not one, not two, but three reports, as it's been a busy week here.

nov. 6 - belle & sebastian, columbus, ohio: . it was the first time belle and sebastian ever played here and the crowd was quite lively, shouting out song titles in between tunes and so forth. and the band ate it up. at one point they called for requests and got a flood of them. they settled on one and said they needed to get lyrics together. so about five songs later, someone came from backstage with printed lyrics to pass around. and they did "cecilia." you know, the simon and garfunkel number. it was quite nice. there also was a woman up front who kept yelling that she wanted to be a go-go dancer, so they brought her up for a tune. she was quite the ham, doing a bunch of fun moves and living it up. you couldn't help but smile. the band was big, with like 14 people on stage. you might say they were the Polyphonic Twee.


nov. 6 - electric six, columbus, ohio: saw the electric six the same night at a different club. (junior senior was opener but we missed them cuz of the belle & sebastian show). the electric six totally rocked. had a ton of attitude and swagger (singer introduced one as "here's a new song that will blow your d*ck off") and really brought the noise. the singer was really strong, almost reminded me of an eddie vedder or something. (i mean that in a good way.) he had the crowd eating out of his hand, getting them to wave their arms in the air and stuff. at the end of their set they said good night and all, but instead of walking off stage and waiting a minute then coming back for an encore, they stood totally still on stage. never seen anyone do that before. then after a few minutes they started to move again and said something about how they were back for more. we left one song into the encore cuz mrs riko was tuckered out, but i read that they ended with a cover of queen's "radio gaga." would've been fun to see that one.

nov. 9 - Polyphonic Spree, columbus ohio: i saw the spree at the coachella fest earlier this year and they just blew me away. the setting was perfect, and i never thought they could be that good again. but darn it if they didn't blow me away again. they opened with The Sun, and that is just such an explosive thing. gave me chills all over again. there were 24 people crammed on the stage -- horns, keyboards, a choir, theremin player, drummer, percussionist, etc. -- and every single one of them looked and acted like it was the happiest day of their lives. don't know how they get up for shows like that, but whatever it is, i hope they'll share some with me sometime. they played for about 45 minutes, then went off stage. the lead guy's son -- i'd put his age at about 6 -- came out and played a drum solo of sorts, then the band came back on. they played a song from "hedwig and the tiny inch" or whatever it's called, then ended with Holiday. if you've seen them before you know they're a must-see, but you might not know they're worth seeing again and again.



ok, who's next?

Saw The Strokes & Kings of Leon on Friday.

This Tori/Fionna Apple chick, Regina Spektor was out first. She played a few crazy piano songs. She apparantly has this love affair with Julian. She came out during The Strokes encore & did a duet with him. You couldn't hear her over his voice & the guitars

Kings of Leon were out next. This is who I came to see. They rocked out in that "we're too young to know we're famous" kind of way. The crowd knew their songs & shouted out requests. If you haven't heard their CD, it's a must listen. California Waiting is my favorite song...

I saw The Strokes in the same venue 1 1/2 years ago. The crowd was OUT of control this time. Fights, flying beers & crowd surfing...I guess that's what "exposure" brings...
Julian gave us the old "I know you won't believe us, but you guys are the best crowd we've had on tour so far" line
NYC Cops was my favorite song they played because the crowd kept calling for it & when they played it people went wild. Another cool point was when they rolled into 12:51 right out of a song from their 1st CD. Almost like they mixed it in. Good night all around.
