Thank god I found this thread. I love the Avalanches and I too am a sucker for identifying samples etc.

Here are some contributions I don't has been mentioned:
"Since I Left You":
The wonderful "Doooo-doooo-doooo-do-do-do-do-doooooooo"-chorus is from the Doo-Wop gone Soft Rock-group The Duprees. Song is called "The Sky's The Limit".

Little Journey":
There's a female voice singing "do wa ba ba ba pa baa" (or something similar, it's a the end of the track for example) - That's Bebel Gilberto singing on the Towa Tei track "Higher". (Bonus info: Towa Tei was that Japanese DJ nerd with the Buddy Holly glasses in the 90s disco trio Deee-lite (who ended up mashed together with The Beatles on GIMIX, remember?)).

SXG wrote:
At the end of Frontier Psychiatrist there is this little guitar riff. I've heard before in a movie (Cinema Paradiso or The 400 Blows) but I still can't find out what the name of it is or who performs it. HELP!!



I don't know where it's from, but a fun trivia bit is that this guitar riff was also used on the japanese pop group PIZZICATO FIVE's album "Bossa Nova 2001" (from 1993, brilliant album btw) which was produced by Keigo Oyamada - a.k.a. CORNELIUS - whose album "Fantasma" (1997) The Avalanches acknowledged as a huge inspiration for the "SILY" album (as stated in an interview for japanese magazine Beikoku Ongaku).

Here's a snippet from that interview:
Are there many bands that you can relate to? Or that you feel that have a similar relation to you?

Dexter: Just little bits from lots of records (laughs)

Robbie: And for different reasons too. Like when we first started, we really liked the wu-tang clan and the 36 chambers because they're big groups and their records are crazy and they themselves are crazy and we just loved that extreme attitude. it was hiphop/punk music.

Dexter: And then we'd also like bands like My Bloody Valentine, just by the fact that their records sounded so unusual (Buy "Loveless" now and you'll understand! /my remark ) but they're great pop records at the same time. They didn't sound like any other records we ever heard. Definately something different.

Like you said, Wu-tang clan and your music are different. WTC is kinda "strong" but we can say The Avalanches' music is "beautiful".

Robbie: We listened to the Cornelius album "Fantasma" (Buy it now and you'll understand! /my remark ) a lot when we were making our album. That was a big influence in the subtleties and the detail and the small sounds. He took so much care with the little moments and that was something we really wanted to do as well.
