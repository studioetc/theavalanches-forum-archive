Ha ha, I didn't know what Dexter looked like before

So anyway, I went down to the social with a mate, some good Djs, good music, fucking packed and good atmosphere, but no Dexter. Another mate turned up and after a bit of convincing decided to come along to The Key. talked another mate out of doing something else to come along as well. Got up to Kings Cross about half eleven and wandered around for a bit wondering where the fuck the club was, to find that it was down an odd little industrial estate right near where I did work experience 7 years ago, with a load of other banging clubs. It was raining. They made us queue for ages for no apparent reason in the rain and got talking to an Aussie bloke (David do you frequent the forums?- hows the medicine testing going!) who was a laugh and a big Avalanches fan. £10 to get in the club and drinks were extortionate, bar staff rude and trying to rip the punters off constantly and a very fragile dancefloor! One of the glass panels in the floor had a glass dropped on it and it shattered all over the place.

Anyway, Dexter came on at 1am, started off with some weird scratchy waltz that was fucking amazing to watch, and as it got lesss scratchy and more dancey we all had a wicked dance. Cant remember a whole lot of specific things that he played, a few little mixes that I knew already but a lot of stuff I hadn't heard before. The crowd went wild for "Hey Ya". His set lasted ages though and I ran out of steam on the dancing front and was feeling frustratingly sober and tired. It was still good to listen to, just wished I felt a bit more lively in the last half hour or so.

ha ha just remember, at the end of his set they played the dueling banjos or whatever it's called from Deliverance and everyone was being really weird and singing along and dancing- I was and still am confused.

The best thing about the night for me was the reactions of people who hadnt known what to expect. Well I hadnt seen Dexter before but I knew what the music would be like, but the mates who I had persuaded to come out were really glad they came, despite the expense and terrible bar staff. One even said that he had been turned back on to clubbing, after becoming a bit disillusioned before.

oh yeah, millions of people in there were socking wet because it started properly pouring with rain just after I got inside

All in all a really good night, but the venue was let down by its prices and staff, and I was let down by getting all lame and sober too early.

Looking forward to Plastic People though, should be a cooler venue.
