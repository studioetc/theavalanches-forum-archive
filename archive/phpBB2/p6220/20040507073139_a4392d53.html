My first introduction to the Avalanches was some time mid-late '90's. I was drinking with friends at the Espy (Esplanade Hotel St.kilda) (Melbourne, Aus) in the front bar, all these people where filling into the back bar so we went down for a look. At first glance I thought great another dogey wannabe Australian rap group but after a couple of songs I realised they were more than that they where a dogey wannabe Beastie Boys rap group and they even had the audasity to throw it in your face by sampling the beastie boys. Fell in love with the music straight away. I use to work for a small record co back then and I still remember the day one of the guys bought a tape in of their stuff it must of got copied 20 times and then all the bidding and rumors that were going round while the major labels tried to sign them, It was just crazy. Finally they got signed and no one heard of them (exept for the gimix tape that literally messed with peoples minds) for a year or two then they had the balls to bring out a dance album! You can just imagine how their label was sweating after signing a rap band that produced a dance/sample record with no proper vocals. As much as I like their sound now it would have been great to hear what the rap style would have sounded like with full production the fat cash they got and a decent studio. Hears to them bringing out another album no one expects them to make.
