yeah, hmv sell lots of bootlegs. they even sell the "as heard on radio soulwax" boots, don't they?

anyway, i sheepishly admit i bought a copy of "when i met you." i knew it was a bootleg and the band wouldn't get any money from it, but figured what the heck. i got it from a u.s. distributor and it came with a "made in england" sticker on it. it's got a folding insert that has a write-up about the band with some pictures we've all seen before. the write-up compares the mix to 2manydjs "as heard on" and coldcut's "journeys" mixes.

the sound quality is great and the mix is about 15 minutes longer than the "gimix" mp3s i've downloaded from zeroswamp and others. it seems the same tracks are used, just that some bits are extended or have extra loops, etc. can't say whether it was worth the $25 i paid for it, but it's still a fantastic mix and it's fun to listen to it again -- in new light.

since it's a bootleg i'm happy to share. i've still got the .ogg files in my slsk shares but will get around to ripping this to mp3 and put it in the shares, too. might see if i can scan the cover for those who want that, too.

'When I Met You' was at the Tower Records on Sunset Blvd. in Los Angeles yesterday in a pretty prominent spot in the 'New Releases' section - $19.99, and ON SALE for $17.99, meaning someone there has fooled themselves into believing its legitimate.... Tower has been selling the bootleg Soulwax's as well.



It's kind of a bummer for those creative bands and DJs that deserve to make the money - seems like no-one's bootlegging Britney Spears or Linkin Park and getting away with it, but they'll bootleg artists who have a real community, and its usually those artists that lose half their sales to people who download the album before it comes out, or soon after. DJ Shadow and RJD2 had very strong sentiments about the whole thing this last year.

If I was The Avalanches I would be pissed. The bootleg looks (and sounds, from the people on the board) to be really well-produced, but it comes from their hard work and effort. Yes, we Avalanches fans are hungry for any material we can get our hands on, but who in the heck ARE these people with the balls to take someone else's work and make a tremendous amount of profit off of it?
