So I went to see The Avalanches at Field Day yesterday.

I suspect that the set was very much along the lines of the Primavera gig… and it was pretty disappointing. I'm struggling to think of any highlights… the crowd got going with a few bits of afrobeat Fela Kuti and (I think) Tabou Combo, but people were drifting off well before the end, and the place was emptying fast before Frankie Sinatra (which was well received) Subways and a half-hearted halfway out the door play of SILY…

I know no one goes to see the Avalanches DJ expecting it to be super polished, but you do expect decent, interesting tune selection, and some sort of thread/coherence in and around things.

But this started off with noise, was incoherent, messy and poorly conceived. It sounded as though they were making it up as they went, although the similarity with comments about Primavera suggest that this can't have been the case. I wonder if they were originally planning a live show, but weren't ready in time so cobbled this lot up at v short notice…?

I'm glad I went, but it was pretty poor fare… let's hope the album brings a bit of good news.

Simon
